Campus Conundrum

Campus Conundrum is a 3D single-player first-person puzzle game that puts your quick thinking to the test. Solve a variety of puzzles within a time limit to progress through levels. The game keeps it simple with easy-to-grasp mechanics, and it explores a modern theme—'Art made by AI.'

It currently features two primary rooms: one acts as a tutorial, guiding players through the essential skills, while the other serves as the first test, demanding the application of the competencies learned in the tutorial. Players are given a 20-minute time limit to navigate and solve puzzles in this initial room. Failing to complete all the puzzles and exit within the allotted time means restarting from the beginning.

It was made in almost three months for an academic project early 2023 and has since been a finalist on 2023 Portugal's PlayStation Awards, nominated in the following:

  • Most Innovative Game 2023;
  • Best Children's Game 2023;
  • usage of PlayStation® Technology Platforms 2023;


    • The game was created in Unity using C# programming language. Being the single programmer of the game, I handled all aspects of its programming. Here are some of the key elements I worked on:

  • Interaction with the environment/objects
  • Unlocking mechanisms for padlocks/codes
  • Linking between the two levels
  • Implementation of dialogues

    • Role: programmer

    Mon Montesk

    Mon Montesk is an Android game where players 'adopt' a virtual monster and must take care of it. Feeding, bathing, and showing affection are some of the activities required to ensure the monster's well-being. If we treat our monster well, it will eventually ascend to the heaven; otherwise, it will meet its demise directly in hell. In Mon Montesk, the possibilities are endless.

    The game was created in Unity using C# programming language and currently, there are three monsters available to play, with many others in the concept stage. Each monster has its own unique story, appearance, and types of interactions.


    Here are some of the key elements I worked in Mon Montesk:

  • interactions with the monster
  • Connecting actions with the monster's stats
  • Minigames
  • Displaying trophies
  • Unity ADS and notifications

    Bergolis

    Bergolis is a first-person VR puzzle game that has been designed to alleviate and cure phobias of various kinds and degrees. Biofeedback mechanisms are used to make the experience more immersive. The aim is to combine a playful part of the games with a therapeutic aspect that is essential for most people, whether they suffer from phobias or not. To this end, there will be a series of puzzles where the player will gradually be exposed to their phobia, always with the possibility of moving on to a safe environment where they can relax and abstract themselves from what torments them.

    The game was created in Unity using C# programming language. Here are some of the key elements I worked on:

  • VR interactions
  • Pet AI
  • Scenario costumizations
  • UI programming
  • Meditative exercises

    Stack Burger: The Food Tower Challenge

    Stack Burger: The Food Tower Challenge is a android game in which the player has to stack the ingredients of a hamburger in order to complete it. There are two game modes available, the infinite game mode in which the player strives to improve his highscore by stacking as many ingredients as possible. And the leveled game mode, where the player has to follow the recipes that are requested in order to complete the level. There are 15 levels available, with increasing difficulty.


    The game was created in Unity using C# programming language. Being the single programmer for the game, I handled all aspects of its programming. Here are some of the key elements I worked on:

  • Main mechanic of dropping ingredients
  • Tracking of elements on each plate
  • Leaderboard in endless mode

    Joust joust

    Just Joust is a hyper-casual battle micro-game where 2 players control the path of a knight, simulating medieval jousting. Playing this game provides a quick and entertaining experience lasting only a few seconds, with the winner enjoying a moment of euphoria lasting an average of 2.5 seconds.

    In Just Joust, players have 2 seconds to draw a path with their computer mouse in the virtual arena, which their knight will follow when the round begins. After both players draw their paths, they can watch as the knights traverse them, hoping that their strategy proves superior to their opponent's.

    Each match lasts for 10 seconds, and players can engage in as many rounds as they desire using the retry button. The game also features in-game score tracking, fostering friendly rivalry among players.

    The game was created in Unreal Engine using blueprints. Being the single programmer for the game, I handled all aspects of its programming. Here are some of the key elements I worked on:

  • Drawing splines and assigning them to the respective knight.
  • Detecting collision between the 'weapon' and the knight, determining the winner of the battle.
  • Score tracking
